AtkinsSalads

Some things I've been making recently.

Basic Ingredients

Veggies
  • organic pre-washed bag-o-mixed greens
  • organic pre-washed bag-o-spinach
  • sundried tomatoes in herb & oil
    • A huge jar at Costco is only $7.99 and lasts me for about 6 months.
  • fresh mushrooms, sliced thick
  • avocado, chunked

Dressings
  • Drew's Tomato and Basil Dressing (not listed on the website, hmm)
  • Drew's Orange-Sesame Dressing (http://www.chefdrew.com/product_descriptions.htm)
    • These have 1g carbs, and are awesome, and not sweetened out the wazoo like most dressings. I'm looking forward to trying the Rosemary Balsamic and the Tahini ones.)
  • Strata's Steaky Dressing (olive oil, cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, dried shallot (sometimes))
  • Strata's Sun-Dried Tomato Dressing (olive oil, cider vinegar, sun-dried tomatoes in oil)

Cheezy Bits
  • Four-Cheese Mexican pre-shredded cheese
  • Six-Cheese Italian pre-shredded cheese

Dead Critters
  • can-o-chicken, white or mixed
  • chicken breast, chunks or slices, steam poached with Herbs de Provence in the water
  • chicken breast, chunks or slices, steam poached with a clove, some dried orange or lemon peel, and a couple whole cumins in the water
  • frozen shrimp, thawed under running water
  • steak, sliced thin, broiled with sage, file', a dash of Lawry's Seasoned Salt, a pat of non-dairy margarine
  • steak, sliced thin, broiled with fresh rosemary and/or fresh basil from the porch, and non-dairy margarine
  • lamb, sliced thin, broiled with thyme, mint, and olive oil

Shrimp Salad
  • mixed greens
  • avocado
  • shrimp
  • Italian or Mexican cheese
  • Drew's Orange-Sesame Dressing
  • maybe mushrooms, but not usually
  • jicama would be good in this, but I don't generally get it

Steak Salad
  • mixed greens
  • mushrooms
  • steak
  • Strata's Sun-Dried Tomato Dressing or Drew's Tomato Dressing (add sun-dried tomatoes in oil)

Steak Salad 2
  • spinach
  • mushrooms
  • steak
  • Strata's Steaky Dressing

Chicken Salad 1
  • mixed greens
  • half can of chicken
  • Mexican or Italian cheese
  • mushrooms
  • sun-dried tomatoes
  • Drew's Tomato Dressing

Chicken Salad 2
  • steam poached chicken
  • spinach or mixed greens
  • mushrooms
  • Drew's Orange-Sesame Dressing

Lamb Salad
  • spinach (sometimes mixed greens)
  • mushrooms
  • Strata's Steaky Dressing or Drew's Orange-Sesame Dressing
  • black olives (if I think of it in time)

Yow, are we bored yet? Surprise-- no! And so far, I'm not tired of it yet-- things still taste really good.

It's also really EASY to eat Atkins-friendly by doing this, and all it takes is one big bowl and a fork, and sometimes a cutting board and knife. I have a habit of reading my email before I eat anything in the morning, and if I run into something that urgently needs fixing or replying to or whatever, I can often go until mid-afternoon on only a cup of coffee.

Being able to put together the salads easily and quickly saves me from going out to the Indian Buffet place a half-mile away, which used to be my default solution for "oh, I'm out of energy and need to eat quickly". The prewashed organic veggies are in the supermarkets now, and while it might be a little cheaper to buy lettuce and shred it, I know from experience that these last longer and are easier to deal with by far.
