How to use theRiceCooker

What to Cook

  • One unit of "stuff" to 3 units of water, 4 units if the "stuff" is brown rice.
  • Cutting a paper cup down to about 1/3 size makes a good "scoop". One scoop of rice and 3 scoops of water makes about one plate of rice (maybe 2 cups).
  • Beans usually need 1 - 2 days of soaking in the fridge to soften enough for rice cooker cooking. Otherwise they come out crunchy in the middle, ick.
  • Please don't cook meat, poultry, or seafood in it. (in our shared office, I was keeping it veggie)
  • Dried fruit comes out well, but if you add anything too sugary it may caramelize a little or even scorch, depending on how much liquid is in there.

How to Cook

  • Press the little tab in the front down to the "Cook" position. It will latch there. Gently!
  • When the stuff is done, or when the water is used up enough that it gets hot enough, the rice cooker will make a soft "ding" and the switch will pop up to "Warm".
  • Be sure to unplug the rice cooker when you are done keeping things warm, it will not shut off by itself.
  • I don't think you can cause a fire by leaving the rice cooker on warm with either nothing in it or just a little stuff in it, but I don't want to find out.
  • Don't try to cook so much that the liquid comes closer than about an inch from the top, it will bubble over while cooking and be a mess.
  • If you're not sure, put a plate under it! A standard dinner plate gives you a slop-over catcher.
    • If things are bubbling over and out the sides, try propping up the lid a crack. This is not going to do for RICE, since that needs a closed system, but for soups or gumbo, it will be fine.

How to Clean

  • Only use plastic utensils to scoop stuff out of it, stir stuff in it, and generally interact with it.
  • Don't use anything harder than a plastic sponge to clean it, the nonstick coating might come off.
  • Don't get water into the main unit, or immerse it in water. Just clean the lid and the lift-out insert.
